Hydrological System

Understanding water challenges and resilience strategies in Russia Dock Woodlands

Why this matters

Our hydrological analysis shows that the site’s challenge is not simply water shortage, but inefficient water input and management. This means the woodland needs better ways to capture, retain, circulate, and reuse water.

Current challenges

Upstream issues

Sediment accumulation, ecological degradation, and declining pond quality.

Downstream issues

Blocked channels, water leakage, intermittent drying, and weak circulation.

System problem

The problem is not only scarcity, but how water enters and moves through the system.

Our proposed strategy

1. Reactivating the pond ecosystem

Introduce a forebay zone, improve circulation, manage reeds, and reduce sediment build-up to restore ecological quality.

2. Downstream circulation

Re-profile the channel, use stop-log weirs, and create a solar-powered closed-loop circulation system in the dry season.

3. Better water input

Shift from relying only on supply toward capturing rainwater through swales, rain gardens, and distributed SuDS.

Short-, medium-, and long-term vision

Short term

Optimise the existing water supply and improve inflow efficiency.

Medium term

Introduce localised rainwater input systems such as swales and rain gardens.

Long term

Build an integrated and resilient water infrastructure linked to wider urban development.

Key conclusion

This project argues for a shift from seeing the site as suffering from a simple lack of water, to understanding it as a system that needs more effective water organisation, circulation, and reuse.
