Ecology and Biodiversity

Russia Dock Woodlands provides habitat for a wide range of urban wildlife and supports important ecological functions within a densely built environment.

Key Ecological Features

This page communicates the site’s environmental value in a way that is accessible to visitors, volunteers, and community stakeholders.

Birdlife

The woodland supports a range of birds including robins, woodpeckers, and other species that rely on urban green space for shelter and nesting.

Plant Diversity

Native trees, shrubs, and understory plants help maintain habitat quality, strengthen soil systems, and support pollinating species.

Insects and Pollinators

Invertebrates play a critical role in ecological resilience, food webs, and seasonal pollination across the site.

Ecosystem Services

Beyond biodiversity, the woodland delivers wider public value by supporting climate resilience, community wellbeing, and environmental learning.

Carbon Storage
Vegetation helps absorb and store carbon.
Flood Mitigation
Soils and planting help slow surface runoff.
Mental Wellbeing
Access to nature supports health and recovery.
Environmental Education
The site offers everyday learning opportunities.
